Japan exit the group stage with a bruising lesson from Brazil: Sano's early strike gave them the lead and genuine belief, but Casemiro's equaliser midway through the second half steadied the Seleção, and Martinelli's stoppage-time winner was a cruel, late twist that obscured how hard Japan had made it. They regroup now for a Round of 16 tie against either Ivory Coast or Norway on Sun Jul 5 13:00 PT — a more navigable assignment than what just passed, with Japan entering as moderate favourites regardless of which side comes through. Win that, and England or Mexico could await in the quarterfinal, with Argentina or Colombia potentially looming in the semifinal as the true measure of whether this side can threaten the latter stages.
