Qatar's group stage exit arrived with little ceremony, a 1-3 defeat to Bosnia & Herzegovina that laid bare the limitations of a side that rarely threatened to trouble the tournament's upper tier. Behind by two goals before half-time following strikes from Alajbegovic and Abunada, Al-Haydos pulled one back on the stroke of the interval to offer a fleeting flicker of hope, only for Mahmic to extinguish it in the 80th minute. The hosts of 2022 finished bottom of Group B and depart with a single point to their name, a tournament record that reflects, honestly enough, where this squad currently stands among the world's best.
